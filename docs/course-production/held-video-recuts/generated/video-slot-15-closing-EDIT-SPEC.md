- Present terms cleanly
- Identify unresolved decision barriers

## Source-depth contract

Replacement spoken words: 758. Minimum: 740. Source baseline: 827.

Required teaching beats:

- `close-built-earlier`: Discovery, rapport, objection handling, and follow-through make formal agreement easier.
- `formal-agreement`: Closing formalizes a decision built through earlier conversations.
- `complete-handoff`: A complete, motivated, documented handoff makes offer review possible.
- `offer-preparation`: Property facts, comparable information, condition, and seller priorities feed offer preparation.
- `price-gap`: A price objection starts with acknowledgment and a question about the gap.
- `mutual-understanding`: Negotiation seeks written terms both sides understand and accept.
- `written-agreement`: Verbal agreement moves into documents, signatures, ownership review, and closing logistics.
- `seller-remorse`: Seller remorse often points back to a person who did not feel heard.
- `closing-risks`: Family influence, title issues, and competing offers are common closing risks.
- `conversation-impact`: Discovery, empathy, notes, and follow-through affect the quality of the close.
- `checkpoint-transition`: The close directs the learner to the checkpoint and capstone.

Required examples:

- `hoping-for-more`: A seller says the offer is lower than expected.
- `seller-remorse-example`: A seller reconsiders after signing because the decision did not feel understood.
- `family-influence-example`: A family member raises a concern late in the process.
- `title-issue-example`: A lien or unclear ownership interrupts closing.
