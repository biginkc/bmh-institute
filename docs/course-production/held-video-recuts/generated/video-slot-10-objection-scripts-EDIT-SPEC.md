## Preserved lesson contract

Practice adaptable responses for price, timing, trust, competition, and decision-maker concerns.

- Select a script pattern that matches the objection
- Adapt wording without changing its intent
- Use questions to reopen dialogue
- Know when to pause and seek guidance

## Source-depth contract

Replacement spoken words: 2483. Minimum: 1900. Source baseline: 2400.

Required teaching beats:

- `practice-pattern`: Use listen, acknowledge, ask, and redirect as an adaptable practice pattern.
- `price`: Explore price expectations and what the requested number needs to accomplish.
- `competition`: Compare other offers and sale paths without attacking them.
- `delay`: Clarify what must happen before the seller can decide.
- `trust`: Offer verifiable information and respect outside review.
- `condition`: Clarify repairs, inspections, occupants, and property facts.
- `financial-distress`: Collect facts and route legal, loan, title, or credit questions for qualified review.
- `written-terms`: Treat special structures, occupancy, price, timing, and costs as written terms subject to review.
- `pause-and-escalate`: Pause when a question crosses authority or professional-advice boundaries.
- `checkpoint-transition`: The close directs the learner to the checkpoint and next lesson.

- 13:20.833: remove `you get a clean, certain outcome without games`. Describe the review process without promising an outcome.

- 23:18.800: remove `I can bring the loan current, stop the sale, and take over payments so you can breathe again and rebuild your credit`. Pause, avoid legal or credit promises, and route the facts for qualified review.
