SCENE 1: Build capability first
Professional development starts with building capability in your current role. Practice a relevant skill, apply it in the work, and review the result. Reliable execution means using what you know consistently while keeping the work accurate and ready for the next person.

SCENE 2: Use a feedback loop
Ask for specific feedback and listen without becoming defensive. If the feedback is unclear, ask a clarifying question. Then practice the corrected approach, apply it in the work, and return with evidence of what changed.

SCENE 3: Confirm ownership changes
Building capability does not automatically change your assigned responsibilities. Take on increased ownership only after your manager confirms the change and it is documented in your current written role plan. Training examples and another person's duties do not redefine your role.

SCENE 4: Use current written expectations
Your current role sheet, offer letter, and manager define the work assigned to you now. Use those current written expectations when choosing a capability to practice. If an older training example conflicts with them, the current documents and manager direction control.

SCENE 5: Make development concrete
Keep development conversations grounded in current role expectations, one capability to practice, recent feedback to apply, and any proposed ownership that needs written confirmation. This turns development into observable practice and reliable work.
