SCENE 1: Start with the written source
One thing needs to be clear up front. This course does not set your pay. The offer letter, current written compensation plan, and role sheet issued to you tell you what applies.

SCENE 2: Understand what the work owns
This lesson is about the work you own and how the company measures it today. Your scorecard may track conversations, handoffs, record quality, or a team result. It describes the work. It does not create a pay term.

SCENE 3: Keep performance visible
Keep a clear record of your work. Use the current process and the scorecard assigned to you. Plans change. An old video or another person's agreement cannot tell you what applies to you now.

SCENE 4: Ask instead of assuming
If the documents are unclear or seem to disagree, stop and ask your manager. Check the latest versions issued to you. Those documents control. This lesson is only context. It is not a compensation agreement.

SCENE 5: Continue into daily operations
Take the Compensation Engine checkpoint next. Then you'll move into the Operator Playbook and Daily Mission Control.
