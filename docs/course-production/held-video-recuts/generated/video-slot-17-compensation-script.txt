SCENE 1: Start with the written source
Let's clear up the most important point first. This training doesn't set your pay or promise a compensation term. Your current offer letter, written compensation plan, and role sheet define what applies to you.

SCENE 2: Understand what the work owns
The useful part of this lesson is understanding what your work owns and how the company measures it right now. A scorecard might focus on qualified conversations, clean handoffs, accurate records, or a team result. Those are examples of business outcomes. They don't define anyone's compensation.

SCENE 3: Keep performance visible
Make your work easy to see. Follow the current process, keep complete notes, and use the scorecard assigned to your work. Scorecards can change as the business changes, so an old video or someone else's agreement can't tell you what applies today.

SCENE 4: Ask instead of assuming
If anything is unclear, or if two documents seem to conflict, pause and ask your manager. Use the latest offer letter, written plan, and role sheet issued to you. Those written documents control. This lesson gives context, but it isn't a compensation agreement.

Take the Compensation Engine checkpoint next. Then you'll move into the Operator Playbook and Daily Mission Control.
